How to Empathize with Users

You’re officially entering the heart of Figma — where design turns from random boxes into beautifully organized art 💻✨

Let’s break down Frames, Grids, and Layers in a simple, funny, and easy-to-remember way 👇

🧱 1️⃣ FRAMES — “The Containers of Your Design”

💬 What They Are:
Think of Frames as containers or boundaries where your design elements live. In Figma, everything is inside a Frame — buttons, screens, images, even entire pages. You can call it your design house 🏠.

🧠 Example:

If your app screen is a pizza 🍕 —
The Frame is the pizza box (it holds everything together).
The toppings (text, icons, buttons) are inside it.
Without a box, toppings would fall all over the place. 😅

💡 Use Frames for:

  • ✅ Designing app screens (e.g., iPhone 14 frame)
  • ✅ Grouping sections (e.g., header, footer)
  • ✅ Creating reusable layouts
  • ✅ Organizing multiple screens

🎮 Funny Analogy: Frames are like Tupperware containers for your design — you can store different things neatly, label them, and move them around. No mess, no stress 😎.

🧭 2️⃣ GRIDS — “Your Design’s Invisible Ruler”

💬 What They Are:
Grids help you align everything perfectly — like keeping text, buttons, and cards in a straight line. They make your design look neat and balanced, not like your messy desk after lunch 😂

🧠 Types of Grids in Figma:

Type Description Example Use
GridSquares — helps with icons or small elementsPixel-perfect designs
ColumnsVertical divisionsWebsite layouts (like 12-column grid)
RowsHorizontal divisionsDashboards or tables

🪄 Example:
Imagine building a website. You don’t just throw buttons anywhere like confetti 🎉 — you place them along the grid lines so everything aligns beautifully. When your design looks clean and professional, it’s not magic — it’s the grid system doing yoga 🧘‍♀️ behind the scenes.

💬 Funny Way to Remember: Grids are like invisible school notebooks — they make sure you don’t write all over the place. 😆

💡 Pro Tip: Designers often use a 12-column grid for websites — it’s like the “universal secret formula” for balance and flexibility.

🧩 3️⃣ LAYERS — “The Stack of Your Design”

💬 What They Are:
Layers show how your elements are stacked on top of each other. Think of them like pancakes 🥞 — The top pancake (text) covers the syrup (button), which sits on a plate (background).

Every element you add in Figma goes into the Layers panel (on the left side).

🧠 Example:

                Profile Card
                ├── Name (Text)
                ├── Profile Picture (Image)
                └── Background (Rectangle)

If you drag the “Background” above the “Name,” the name disappears behind it — like someone hiding behind a curtain 😆

💬 Funny Analogy: Layers are like bedsheets — if you put the blanket (background) on top of yourself (text), nobody will see you! 😂 So, always stack your layers wisely.

🧠 Why These Three Matter Together

Feature Purpose Fun Comparison
FramesOrganize and hold your designPizza box 🍕
GridsKeep design aligned and cleanNotebook lines 📘
LayersStack elements properlyPancake stack 🥞

When used together → your design looks structured, professional, and satisfying. ✨

🧩 Real-Life Example:

Imagine you’re designing the Spotify mobile app 🎧

  • Each screen (Home, Search, Library) = a Frame
  • Each section (song cards, buttons) follows a Grid
  • All elements (text, icons, backgrounds) stack properly in Layers

Result? A neat, responsive, and user-friendly app that feels effortless to use. 🎶

🧭 Quick Recap:

Concept Description Analogy
FramesContainers for your designPizza boxes 🍕
GridsGuides for alignmentNotebook lines 📘
LayersOrder of elementsPancake stack 🥞

💬 In Short:

Frames keep things together.
Grids keep things in line.
Layers keep things in order.

Together, they make your design tidy, readable, and absolutely delightful! 🎨💥
