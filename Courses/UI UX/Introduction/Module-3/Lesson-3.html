Typography Essentials

🅰️ What is Typography?

Typography is the art and technique of arranging text so it’s readable, clear, and visually appealing.

It’s not just what you write, but how you show it — your design’s tone of voice.

“Welcome!” feels friendly — while “WELCOME!!!” feels like someone’s yelling

Why Typography Matters

Typography guides how people feel and read your content. Good typography makes your message easy and enjoyable to read.

Bad typography makes people scroll away faster than you can say "Comic Sans." 😂

The Anatomy of Typography

Think of fonts like characters in a movie — each has its own personality.

1. Serif Fonts

These have little decorative feet (serifs) at the end of letters.

Feels: Traditional, trustworthy, formal.

Example: Times New Roman, Georgia.

2. Sans-Serif Fonts

Clean and simple — no decorative ends.

Feels: Modern, friendly, easy to read.

Example: Arial, Helvetica, Poppins.

3. Script Fonts

Look like handwriting or calligraphy — elegant and expressive.

Feels: Creative, romantic, stylish.

Example: Pacifico, Great Vibes.

4. Display Fonts

Bold and decorative — perfect for attention-grabbing headlines.

Feels: Loud, fun, and unique.

Example: Impact, Lobster, Bangers.

Typography Rules (Made Simple)

1. Hierarchy

Make important text look important using size, weight, or color.

Example: Title > Subtitle > Body — like a family hierarchy

2. Contrast

Use two complementary fonts. Bold sans-serif + light serif = chef’s kiss

Avoid mixing too many — Comic Sans + Gothic + Cursive = chaos

3. Alignment

Choose one alignment (left, center, or right). Stick to it for consistency.

Tip: Left-aligned is easiest to read for long paragraphs.

4. Spacing

Kerning = space between letters. Leading = between lines. Tracking = overall word spacing.

Good spacing = happy eyes

5. Consistency

Stick to 2–3 fonts max per design. More than that = identity crisis

Color + Typography = Emotion Multiplier 💥

The combo of font and color changes how people feel about your design.

  • Big Bold + Red → Energy, power, attention
  • Soft Script + Pink → Romance, calmness
  • Clean Sans + Blue → Professional, trustworthy

🎯 Funny Way to Remember It

Typography is like dressing your words:

  • Serif = Suit
  • Sans-serif = Jeans & T-shirt
  • Script = Fancy dress
  • Display = Costume for a party

Dress your message for the right occasion!
